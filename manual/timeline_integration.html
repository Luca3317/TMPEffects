Timeline integration

TMPEffects is integrated with Unity's Timeline package, providing its own timeline clips and markers.

Clips

At the moment, TMPEffects provides two different clip types.

TMPAnimationClip

The TMPAnimationClip plays an animation (any asset implementing ITMPAnimation works) while it is active.
It additionally allows you to blend the animation in and out, as well as pre/post extrapolating it.

TMPMeshModifier clip

The TMPMeshModifierClip allows you to apply one-time mesh modifications that aren't necessarily suited to periodic animations.
For example, at some specific point in your cutscene, you might want your text to change color or jump up once.

Using an TMPMeshModifierClip you may modify the character's positions, rotation and scale, as well as position, color and alpha of the individual vertices. Blending and extrapolation is supported.
You may also blend in from a different set of modifiers, using the Initial Modifiers option in the clip inspector, or use the Wave option to continuously blend the modifiers (more about Waves here).

Markers

Custom markers allow you to control a TMPWriter and TMPAnimator by calling their most common / important methods.
Be aware that you will need a TMPWriterMarkReceiver or TMPAnimatorMarkReceiver component respectively on the same GameObject in order for these markers to work.
For TMPWriter, the following methods have corresponding timeline markers:

  • StartWriter()
  • StopWriter()
  • ResetWriter(int)
  • SkipWriter(bool)
  • RestartWriter(int)
  • Wait(float)
  • SetSkippable(bool)
  • ResetWait()

For TMPAnimator, the following methods have corresponding timeline markers:

  • StartAnimation()
  • StopAnimation()
  • UpdateAnimations(float)
  • SetUpdateFrom(UpdateFrom)
  • ResetAnimations()
  • ResetAnimations(float)
